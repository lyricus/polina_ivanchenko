☕ Coffee Culture

Best Coffee Spots in Ljubljana

Ljubljana takes its coffee seriously. These are not chains — they are local institutions where students come to work, meet, and feel at home.

Student Favourite

Črno Zrno

Translated as "Black Grain," this cosy specialty café near the Faculty of Arts is a student institution. Superb espresso, friendly baristas who remember your order, and the best croissants in the city. Free Wi-Fi, plenty of plug sockets, and a relaxed vibe that welcomes long study sessions.

📍 Rimska cesta 4 ⏰ Mon–Fri 7:30–21:00
💶 Espresso ~€2.10 📶 Free Wi-Fi
Cultural Spot

TOZD

Located in the Metelkova cultural district, TOZD is more than a café — it is a community hub with live music evenings, art exhibitions, and some of the most interesting people in Ljubljana. Excellent natural wines alongside great coffee. Come in the afternoon, stay until midnight.

📍 Masarykova ulica 24 ⏰ Tue–Sun 10:00–01:00
💶 Coffee ~€2.50 🎵 Live events weekly
Best Outdoor Seating

Cafetino

Perched right on the Ljubljanica riverbank, Cafetino is the place for an afternoon coffee with a view. Colourful chairs spill onto the terrace in spring and summer — reserve a spot early on sunny days. Great for meeting new friends and watching the city go by.

📍 Gallusovo nabrežje 27 ⏰ Daily 8:00–22:00
💶 Cappuccino ~€2.80 ☀️ River terrace
Local tip 🇸🇮 — In Ljubljana, coffee culture is social. Do not rush. Ordering a "kava" (coffee) and sitting for two hours is completely normal and expected. Baristas will never ask you to leave. This is not a take-away city.
🗺 Must-See Places

Ljubljana's Best Spots

A compact city full of character. These are the places every new resident should visit in their first month.

Must Visit

Ljubljana Castle (Ljubljanski Grad)

The medieval castle crowning the hill at the heart of the city. Take the funicular (€4 return, free with student card) or hike up through the forest in 15 minutes. The panoramic view of the city, the Alps, and on clear days even the Adriatic coast is unforgettable. Free entry to the grounds — paid entry for the tower and exhibitions.

Tivoli Park

Ljubljana's green lung — running, picnics, and peace

Triple Bridge (Tromostovje)

The iconic symbol of Ljubljana — three bridges, one view

Metelkova Mesto

Europe's most creative autonomous cultural zone — open to everyone

Open-Air Market (Tržnica)

Fresh local produce by the river every morning, Mon–Sat

Opening hours tip ⚠️ — Most Ljubljana museums are closed on Mondays. Plan cultural visits for Tuesday through Sunday. The castle and parks are always open.
🚲 Getting Around

Transport in Ljubljana

Good news: Ljubljana is tiny. Most things are walkable. Here is what you need to know about the rest.

🚌

Bus (LPP)

Ljubljana's city bus network covers the whole city. All buses are modern and air-conditioned. You cannot pay with cash on board — you must use an Urbana card or the LPP mobile app.

  • → Single ride: €1.30 with Urbana
  • → Day pass: €3.50
  • → Monthly student pass: ~€23
  • → Real-time: lpp.si or Moovit app
💳

Urbana Card

The rechargeable smart card used for all buses, the funicular, and some parking. Get yours at any Urbanija kiosk (in LPP bus shelters) or at the LPP customer centre at Slovenska 56. The card itself costs €2.

  • → Buy at Urbanija kiosks citywide
  • → Top up: kiosks, app, or online
  • → Register your card to protect balance
  • → Student discount requires enrolment proof
🚲

BicikeLJ Bikes

The city's public bike-sharing system with 36 stations across the city centre. The first hour of every ride is free. Perfect for short trips — from the train station to university, from the market to Tivoli Park.

  • → First hour: FREE every ride
  • → Annual subscription: €9/year
  • → Register at bicikelj.si
  • → Helmets not provided — bring your own
🚶

Walking

Ljubljana's old town is entirely car-free. Most students walk everywhere in the centre. From the central train station to the castle hill is only 15 minutes on foot along some of the most beautiful streets in Central Europe.

  • → Old town: fully pedestrianised
  • → River promenade: scenic 30-min walk
  • → Download Maps.me for offline navigation
  • → City centre is flat and very walkable
🚆

Trains & Intercity

Ljubljana's central train station (Železniška postaja) connects to all major Slovenian cities and neighbouring countries. For day trips: Bled (1h), Piran (2h), Maribor (2h). Book at potniski.sz.si for the best student prices.

  • → Student 50% discount with ISIC or enrolment
  • → Intercity bus: Nomago, FlixBus
  • → Book online to save 20–30%
🚕

Taxis & Rideshare

Use the Hopin app (local) or Bolt for fair-priced taxis. Avoid hailing unmarked taxis at the station — prices can be 3× higher. Hopin averages €5–8 for most city-centre trips.

  • → Hopin: cheapest and most reliable
  • → Bolt: also available, good late at night
  • → Airport taxi: book in advance (~€28 fixed)
