📋 Priority Checklist

Your first week in Ljubljana

Do these five things in your first seven days. Everything else can wait.

1

Register your temporary residence

Go to the Administrative Unit (Upravna enota Ljubljana) at Tobačna ulica 5 with your passport and proof of address (rental contract or hotel booking). This registers you as a legal resident. Required for healthcare, banking, and most official processes. Opens Mon–Fri 8:00–13:00.

2

Get a Slovenian SIM card

Telekom Slovenia (Trg republike 3) and A1 (Nazorjeva 2) both sell prepaid SIMs with good data plans. Bring your passport. A prepaid SIM with 15 GB of data costs around €15/month. You need this for the LPP bus app, BicikeLJ bikes, and most digital services.

3

Open a bank account

Delavska hranilnica and N26 / Revolut (app-based) are easiest for newcomers. N26 and Revolut require no Slovenian address. For a local account, NLB and SKB banks are student-friendly — bring your passport, residence registration, and enrolment certificate.

4

Choose a personal physician (osebni zdravnik)

You can see a doctor without one, but having a registered personal physician means faster appointments and lower costs. Find the list of available doctors at zzzs.si. Call or visit the practice to register — bring your health insurance card (or temporary certificate) and residence proof.

5

Get your student card and Urbana card

Your university will issue you a student card — activate it immediately as it gives you access to the student dining card (subvencija za prehrano) which provides subsidised meals at €3–4 per meal at over 40 restaurants. Get your Urbana bus card at any LPP kiosk (€2 for the card).

🏛 Documents & Admin

Documents you will need

Always carry copies of these. Originals in your bag, photos on your phone.

🛂

For Ukrainian citizens (TZ status)

  • Temporary protection certificate (Začasna zaščita) — the most important document
  • Ukrainian passport (biometric preferred but not required)
  • Proof of accommodation (rental contract / invitation letter)
  • Apply for TZ status within 3 days of arrival at Policijska uprava Ljubljana
  • Status valid 1 year, renewable — gives you full rights to work, study, and access healthcare
🏫

University enrolment documents

  • Ukrainian diploma / school leaving certificate (apostilled or notarised)
  • Certified translation into Slovenian or English
  • Language certificate (B2 Slovenian or B2 English depending on programme)
  • Transcript of records from your previous institution
  • Contact ENIC-NARIC Slovenia (uznanje.si) for credential recognition — it takes 1–3 months
💰

Financial support for Ukrainian students

  • Slovenian government waives tuition fees for Ukrainian students under TZ protection
  • Monthly social assistance: up to €402/month for single adults (apply at CSD Ljubljana)
  • Student subsidised meals: ~€3.50/meal at participating restaurants
  • UNHCR emergency fund: contact UNHCR Ljubljana for one-time assistance
  • Caritas Slovenia: food packages, clothing vouchers, counselling
🏠

Housing & accommodation

  • Student dormitories (studentski domovi): apply at stud-dom.si — very limited spots
  • Private rooms: njuskalo.si, nepremicnine.net, Facebook groups "Stanovanje Ljubljana"
  • Average room rent: €350–550/month including bills
  • Rental contracts must be registered — ask your landlord, it is their legal obligation
  • Emergency shelter: contact Slovenska filantropija (filantropija.si)
Important ⚠️ — Always make photocopies AND phone photos of every official document. Store them in a secure cloud (Google Drive, iCloud) so you can access them even if your bag is lost or stolen.
🗣 Language

Essential Slovenian phrases

Most Slovenians under 40 speak good English. But learning a few words will earn you a genuine smile — and sometimes a free coffee.

Slovenian Ukrainian / Meaning Pronunciation tip
Zdravo / Živjo Привіт / Hello (informal) "ZDRA-vo" / "ZHEE-vyo"
Dober dan Доброго дня / Good day "DOH-ber dahn"
Hvala Дякую / Thank you "HVAH-lah" — very common, use it always
Prosim Будь ласка / Please / You're welcome "PROH-seem" — also means "yes please" and "here you go"
Oprostite Вибачте / Excuse me / Sorry "oh-PRO-stee-teh"
Koliko stane? Скільки коштує? / How much is it? "KOH-lee-koh STAH-neh"
Kje je ...? Де знаходиться ...? / Where is ...? "kyeh yeh" — point at your phone map if unsure
Ne razumem Я не розумію / I don't understand "neh rah-ZOO-mem"
Govorite angleško? Ви говорите англійською? / Do you speak English? "go-VOH-ree-teh AHN-glesh-koh"
Nasvidenje До побачення / Goodbye "nahs-VEE-den-yeh"
Ena kava, prosim Одну каву, будь ласка / One coffee please "EH-nah KAH-vah PRO-seem" — the most important sentence
Živeli! Будьмо! / Cheers! "ZHEE-ve-lee" — always look people in the eyes when toasting
Fun fact 🇸🇮 — Slovenian and Ukrainian are both Slavic languages, so many words look similar written down! "Dom" (home), "voda" (water), "hleb/kruh" (bread), "mleko" (milk) — you already know more Slovenian than you think.
Where to learn more: Free Slovenian classes for newcomers are offered by ISA (Institute for Slovenian Language) and various NGOs including Slovenska filantropija and Karitas. Ask at your university international office.
🏥 Healthcare

Health & medical care

Slovenia has an excellent public healthcare system. Under temporary protection (TZ), you have the same rights as Slovenian citizens.

💊

General healthcare

  • Register with a personal physician (osebni zdravnik) within your first month
  • For urgent non-emergency care: go to a "nujni ambulanta" without appointment
  • Nearest 24h urgent care: Urgentni center Ljubljana, Zaloška 2
  • Prescriptions (recepti) from your doctor can be filled at any pharmacy (lekarna)
  • ZZZS health card — apply for yours at ZZZS office, Miklošičeva 24
🧠

Mental health & wellbeing

  • Moving to a new country is hard. Seeking support is brave, not weak.
  • Free psychological counselling for Ukrainians: Karitas (karitas.si), info line 031 700 100
  • University of Ljubljana student counselling: svetovalnica.uni-lj.si
  • 24h crisis line (in Slovenian/English): 116 123 (Zaupni telefon Samarijan)
  • Online Ukrainian-language therapy: uahelp.me (free for displaced Ukrainians)
🏋️

Sports & physical activity

  • Student sports card (Športna izkaznica) gives discounted gym access city-wide
  • Free outdoor gym equipment in Tivoli Park — open 24/7
  • University sports (ŠOU): basketball, volleyball, swimming — very affordable
  • Cycling and running paths connect all major parks — completely free
  • BTC City has a large free ice-skating rink in winter
💉

Vaccinations & documentation

  • If you have Ukrainian vaccination records, bring them — they are usually recognised
  • COVID-19 vaccination is no longer required for entry or services in Slovenia
  • If records are lost: NIJZ (national health institute) can issue a vaccination plan
  • Flu vaccine is free every October at your personal physician's practice
🎓 Student Life

Student services & organisations

Ljubljana is a student city. These organisations exist specifically to help you.

🎓

ŠOU — Student Organisation

The main student union. Manages student dining cards (subvencija), organises cultural events, and provides legal and social support. Their office is at Kersnikova 4, Ljubljana. Website: sou-lj.si

Visit ŠOU →
🌍

International Student Club

AEGEE Ljubljana and ESN Slovenia organise regular events specifically for international students: language cafés, city tours, weekend trips, and intercultural evenings. A great way to make local friends fast.

ESN Slovenia →
🍽️

Student Dining Card

With a valid student card you get a meal coupon worth €3.50 per meal at 40+ partner restaurants citywide. Activate your card at ŠOU and load coupons via the eŠtudentServis app. This will save you hundreds of euros per semester.

eŠtudentServis →
📚

City & University Libraries

NUK (National and University Library) is free with student ID — one of the most beautiful buildings in Ljubljana. Great study spaces, fast Wi-Fi, and access to international academic databases. Also great for a quiet afternoon.

🤝

Cultural Bridge Programme

That is us! Our programme pairs Ukrainian students with local Slovenian "ambassadors" for the first semester — language practice, city tours, and honest advice about student life. Fill in the form below to get matched.

💼

Part-time Work Rights

Under TZ protection, you have the right to work in Slovenia without a special permit. Student jobs (študentsko delo) via eŠtudentServis are tax-advantaged. Average hourly rate: €8–12. Popular sectors: hospitality, retail, tutoring.
