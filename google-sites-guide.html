You are not alone in this city
Cultural Bridge Ambassadors is a student-led initiative connecting Ukrainian newcomers with local Slovenian students in Ljubljana. We believe that the best way to feel at home is through shared experiences, honest information, and genuine friendship. This guide was created by young people who have gone through the same questions you are asking right now: Where do I get a SIM card? Which coffee shop has the fastest Wi-Fi? How do I use the Urbana bus card? We have answered all of these — and more. Whether you arrived last week or last year, this is your starting point. Explore the city, reach out, and let us help you build your Ljubljana story.

Črno Zrno Translated as "Black Grain," this cosy specialty café near the Faculty of Arts is a student institution. Superb espresso, friendly baristas who remember your order, and the best croissants in the city. Free Wi-Fi, plenty of plug sockets, and a relaxed vibe that welcomes long study sessions. 📍 Rimska cesta 4 · ⏰ Mon–Fri 7:30–21:00 · 💶 Espresso ~€2.10 · 📶 Free Wi-Fi
TOZD Located in the Metelkova cultural district, TOZD is more than a café — it is a community hub with live music evenings, art exhibitions, and some of the most interesting people in Ljubljana. Excellent natural wines alongside great coffee. 📍 Masarykova ulica 24 · ⏰ Tue–Sun 10:00–01:00 · 💶 Coffee ~€2.50 · 🎵 Live events weekly
Cafetino Perched right on the Ljubljanica riverbank, Cafetino is the place for an afternoon coffee with a view. Colourful chairs spill onto the terrace in spring and summer — reserve a spot early on sunny days. Great for meeting new friends and watching the city go by. 📍 Gallusovo nabrežje 27 · ⏰ Daily 8:00–22:00 · 💶 Cappuccino ~€2.80 · ☀️ River terrace

🚌 Bus (LPP) All buses are modern and air-conditioned. You cannot pay with cash on board — use an Urbana card or the LPP mobile app. • Single ride: €1.30 with Urbana • Day pass: €3.50 • Monthly student pass: ~€23 • Real-time info: lpp.si or Moovit app 💳 Urbana Card The rechargeable smart card for all buses, the funicular, and parking. Get yours at any Urbanija kiosk (in LPP bus shelters) or at Slovenska 56. The card itself costs €2. • Top up: kiosks, app, or online • Register your card to protect your balance • Student discount requires enrolment proof 🚲 BicikeLJ Bikes The city's public bike-sharing system with 36 stations. The first hour of every ride is FREE. • Annual subscription: €9/year • Register at bicikelj.si 🚶 Walking Ljubljana's old town is entirely car-free. From the central train station to the castle hill is only 15 minutes on foot. Download Maps.me for offline navigation. 🚆 Trains & Intercity To Bled: 1h · To Piran: 2h · To Maribor: 2h Student 50% discount with ISIC or enrolment proof. Book at potniski.sz.si 🚕 Hopin App (Taxi) Use Hopin (local) or Bolt for fair-priced taxis. Avoid unmarked taxis at the station. Average city trip: €5–8.

11 📋 First Week Checklist Numbered List
Как добавить: Insert → Text Block → выбери стиль Numbered List
Your First Week in Ljubljana — Do These 5 Things 1. Register your temporary residence Go to the Administrative Unit (Upravna enota Ljubljana) at Tobačna ulica 5 with your passport and proof of address. Opens Mon–Fri 8:00–13:00. 2. Get a Slovenian SIM card Telekom Slovenia (Trg republike 3) or A1 (Nazorjeva 2). Bring your passport. ~€15/month for 15 GB data. 3. Open a bank account N26 or Revolut (app-based, no Slovenian address needed) are easiest. For local account: NLB or SKB banks. 4. Choose a personal physician (osebni zdravnik) Find available doctors at zzzs.si. Call or visit the practice — bring health insurance card and residence proof. 5. Get your student card and Urbana card Activate your student card for subsidised meals (~€3.50/meal at 40+ restaurants). Urbana bus card: €2 at any LPP kiosk.
